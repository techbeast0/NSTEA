Anticoagulation Therapy Management
Source: CHEST/ASH 2024 Anticoagulation Guidelines (summarized)

WARFARIN MANAGEMENT
- Target INR: 2.0-3.0 for most indications (atrial fibrillation, VTE)
- Target INR: 2.5-3.5 for mechanical heart valves
- Initial dose: 5mg daily, adjust based on INR response
- Monitoring: INR every 2-3 days initially, then weekly, then monthly when stable
- Maximum therapeutic INR range: within 0.5 of target

WARFARIN DRUG INTERACTIONS (CRITICAL)
These drugs INCREASE warfarin effect (bleeding risk):
- Antibiotics: metronidazole, fluconazole, TMP-SMX, ciprofloxacin, azithromycin
- NSAIDs: ibuprofen, naproxen (also increase bleeding via platelet inhibition)
- Acetaminophen: in doses > 2g/day, increases INR
- Amiodarone: dramatically increases warfarin levels — reduce warfarin dose 30-50%
- Omeprazole/esomeprazole: moderate CYP2C19 interaction

These drugs DECREASE warfarin effect:
- Rifampin: strong CYP inducer — may need 2-3x warfarin dose
- Carbamazepine, phenytoin, phenobarbital
- Vitamin K (dietary or supplements)

WARFARIN + NSAID — ABSOLUTE AVOID
- Combined anticoagulant + antiplatelet effect = dramatically increased GI and intracranial bleeding
- If analgesia needed: use acetaminophen (< 2g/day)
- If anti-inflammatory needed: short course with PPI gastroprotection + close INR monitoring

DIRECT ORAL ANTICOAGULANTS (DOAC)
- Apixaban (Eliquis): 5mg BID (reduce to 2.5mg BID if elderly + low weight + CrCl concern)
- Rivaroxaban (Xarelto): 20mg daily with food
- Dabigatran (Pradaxa): 150mg BID (reduce if CrCl 30-50)
- Edoxaban (Savaysa): 60mg daily (reduce to 30mg if CrCl 15-50 or weight < 60kg)

DOAC ADVANTAGES OVER WARFARIN
- No routine INR monitoring needed
- Fewer drug-food interactions
- Lower intracranial hemorrhage risk
- Fixed dosing

SWITCHING BETWEEN AGENTS
- Warfarin to DOAC: Start DOAC when INR < 2.0
- DOAC to warfarin: Start warfarin, continue DOAC for 3-5 days until INR therapeutic
- Bridging with LMWH: only for mechanical valves or high-risk situations

CHA2DS2-VASc SCORE (for AF anticoagulation decision)
- C: CHF (+1)
- H: Hypertension (+1)
- A2: Age >= 75 (+2)
- D: Diabetes (+1)
- S2: Stroke/TIA history (+2)
- V: Vascular disease (+1)
- A: Age 65-74 (+1)
- Sc: Sex category — female (+1)
Score >= 2 (men) or >= 3 (women): recommend anticoagulation
