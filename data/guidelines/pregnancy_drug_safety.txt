Drug Safety in Pregnancy
Source: FDA Pregnancy Categories and ACOG Guidelines (summarized)

CATEGORY X — ABSOLUTELY CONTRAINDICATED
These drugs cause fetal harm; risk clearly outweighs any benefit:
- Isotretinoin (Accutane): severe birth defects (craniofacial, cardiac, CNS)
- Methotrexate: embryotoxic and teratogenic
- Warfarin: warfarin embryopathy (nasal hypoplasia, stippled epiphyses), CNS defects
- Statins (all): potential teratogenicity — discontinue before conception
- Thalidomide: limb defects (phocomelia)
- Misoprostol: uterine contractions, fetal death
- Finasteride/Dutasteride: abnormal male fetal genitalia
- Ribavirin: teratogenic — requires two forms of contraception
- Leflunomide: teratogenic — requires washout with cholestyramine

CATEGORY D — EVIDENCE OF RISK, use only if benefit outweighs risk
- ACE inhibitors/ARBs: renal agenesis, oligohydramnios (especially 2nd/3rd trimester)
- Phenytoin: fetal hydantoin syndrome
- Valproic acid: neural tube defects, developmental delay
- Lithium: Ebstein anomaly (low risk), neonatal complications
- Tetracyclines: tooth discoloration, bone growth inhibition
- Aminoglycosides: ototoxicity

SAFE IN PREGNANCY (commonly used)
- Acetaminophen: safe analgesic/antipyretic
- Penicillins and cephalosporins: safe antibiotics
- Insulin: safe for glucose control (preferred over oral agents)
- Labetalol: safe for hypertension
- Nifedipine: safe for hypertension and tocolysis
- Methyldopa: safe for hypertension (long track record)
- Ondansetron: generally considered safe for nausea (limited data)
- Prenatal vitamins with folic acid: recommended

MEDICATIONS TO AVOID
- NSAIDs: avoid especially in 3rd trimester (premature closure of ductus arteriosus)
- Fluoroquinolones: cartilage damage risk
- Trimethoprim: folate antagonist (avoid in 1st trimester)
- Live vaccines: contraindicated

TREATMENT ALTERNATIVES
- Pain: acetaminophen instead of NSAIDs
- Hypertension: labetalol/nifedipine instead of ACE/ARB
- Diabetes: insulin instead of metformin/sulfonylureas
- Anticoagulation: LMWH (enoxaparin) instead of warfarin
- Acne: topical treatments instead of isotretinoin
- Depression: sertraline or fluoxetine (SSRIs with most safety data)
