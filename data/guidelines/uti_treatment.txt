Urinary Tract Infection (UTI) Treatment Guidelines
Source: IDSA/AUA 2023 Guidelines (summarized)

UNCOMPLICATED UTI (CYSTITIS) IN WOMEN
First-line agents:
- Nitrofurantoin monohydrate 100mg BID x 5 days
- Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole (TMP-SMX) 160/800mg BID x 3 days (if local resistance < 20%)
- Fosfomycin trometamol 3g single dose

Second-line agents (if first-line not suitable):
- Ciprofloxacin 250mg BID x 3 days
- Levofloxacin 250mg daily x 3 days
- Beta-lactams: amoxicillin-clavulanate, cefdinir, cephalexin (less effective)

COMPLICATED UTI / PYELONEPHRITIS
- Fluoroquinolone (ciprofloxacin or levofloxacin) x 7-14 days
- TMP-SMX x 14 days (if susceptible)
- Third-generation cephalosporin (ceftriaxone) IV if hospitalized

ALLERGY CONSIDERATIONS
- Penicillin allergy: Safe alternatives include nitrofurantoin, TMP-SMX, fluoroquinolones, fosfomycin
- Sulfa allergy: Avoid TMP-SMX, use nitrofurantoin, fluoroquinolones, fosfomycin, or cephalosporins
- Fluoroquinolone contraindication (tendon rupture risk): Use nitrofurantoin or cephalosporins

RENAL DOSING
- Nitrofurantoin: AVOID if CrCl < 30 mL/min (ineffective, risk of toxicity)
- TMP-SMX: Reduce dose if CrCl 15-30 mL/min; avoid if < 15
- Fluoroquinolones: Dose adjustment needed for CrCl < 30

SPECIAL POPULATIONS
- Pregnancy: Nitrofurantoin (avoid near term), cephalexin, amoxicillin (if susceptible)
- Pregnancy AVOID: Fluoroquinolones, TMP-SMX (first trimester folate antagonism, third trimester kernicterus)
- Elderly: Prefer nitrofurantoin or TMP-SMX; avoid fluoroquinolones if possible (C. diff risk)
