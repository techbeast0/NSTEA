Hypertension Management Guidelines
Source: ACC/AHA 2024 Blood Pressure Guidelines (summarized)

CLASSIFICATION
- Normal: < 120/80 mmHg
- Elevated: 120-129 / < 80 mmHg
- Stage 1 HTN: 130-139 / 80-89 mmHg
- Stage 2 HTN: >= 140/90 mmHg
- Hypertensive Crisis: > 180/120 mmHg

FIRST-LINE MEDICATIONS
- ACE inhibitors: lisinopril, enalapril, ramipril
- ARBs: losartan, valsartan, irbesartan (alternative if ACE intolerant due to cough)
- Calcium channel blockers: amlodipine, nifedipine
- Thiazide diuretics: hydrochlorothiazide, chlorthalidone

COMBINATION THERAPY
- Preferred: ACE/ARB + CCB or ACE/ARB + thiazide
- Do NOT combine ACE + ARB (increased renal risk, no added benefit)
- Triple therapy if needed: ACE/ARB + CCB + thiazide

SPECIAL POPULATIONS
- Diabetes: ACE/ARB first-line (renal protection). Target < 130/80
- CKD with proteinuria: ACE/ARB mandatory. Target < 130/80
- Heart failure (HFrEF): ACE/ARB + beta-blocker + diuretic + MRA
- Pregnancy: labetalol, nifedipine, or methyldopa. AVOID ACE/ARB (teratogenic)
- Black patients: CCB or thiazide preferred as initial therapy

HYPERTENSIVE EMERGENCY (> 180/120 with end-organ damage)
- IV labetalol, nicardipine, or nitroprusside
- Target: reduce MAP by 25% in first hour
- Avoid rapid or excessive BP reduction

DRUG INTERACTIONS AND CAUTIONS
- ACE inhibitors + potassium-sparing diuretics: hyperkalemia risk
- NSAIDs reduce effectiveness of all antihypertensives
- ACE inhibitors: monitor creatinine and potassium after initiation
- Beta-blockers: avoid abrupt discontinuation (rebound hypertension)

MONITORING
- Recheck BP 1 month after starting/changing therapy
- Annual labs: basic metabolic panel, lipid panel
- Home BP monitoring recommended for all patients on therapy
