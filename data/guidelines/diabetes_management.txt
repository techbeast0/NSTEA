Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus Management Guidelines
Source: ADA Standards of Care 2024 (summarized)

DIAGNOSIS
- HbA1c >= 6.5%
- Fasting plasma glucose >= 126 mg/dL
- 2-hour plasma glucose >= 200 mg/dL during OGTT
- Random glucose >= 200 mg/dL with classic symptoms

GLYCEMIC TARGETS
- General: HbA1c < 7.0%
- Less stringent (elderly, comorbidities): HbA1c < 8.0%
- More stringent (young, no complications): HbA1c < 6.5%
- Fasting glucose: 80-130 mg/dL
- Postprandial: < 180 mg/dL

FIRST-LINE THERAPY
- Metformin 500mg once daily, titrate to 1000mg BID over 1-2 months
- Contraindicated if eGFR < 30 mL/min; reduce dose if eGFR 30-45
- Hold metformin 48 hours before/after iodinated contrast procedures
- Side effects: GI (nausea, diarrhea) — take with food; rare lactic acidosis

SECOND-LINE THERAPY (if HbA1c not at goal after 3 months on metformin)
- SGLT2 inhibitors (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin): preferred if CVD, HF, or CKD
- GLP-1 receptor agonists (semaglutide, liraglutide): preferred if CVD or obesity
- DPP-4 inhibitors (sitagliptin, linagliptin): if cost or simplicity is priority
- Sulfonylureas (glimepiride, glipizide): effective but hypoglycemia risk
- Insulin: if HbA1c > 10% or symptomatic hyperglycemia at diagnosis

CARDIOVASCULAR RISK MANAGEMENT
- Statin therapy: moderate-intensity for all T2DM age 40-75; high-intensity if ASCVD
- Aspirin 75-162mg: secondary prevention (established CVD)
- BP target: < 130/80 mmHg, ACE/ARB preferred
- SGLT2 inhibitors reduce heart failure hospitalization and CKD progression

MONITORING
- HbA1c every 3 months until stable, then every 6 months
- Annual: lipid panel, urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio, serum creatinine/eGFR
- Annual dilated eye exam, comprehensive foot exam
- Self-monitoring blood glucose (SMBG) as needed

HYPOGLYCEMIA MANAGEMENT
- Glucose < 70 mg/dL: 15-20g fast-acting carbs, recheck in 15 minutes
- Severe hypoglycemia: glucagon kit; reassess regimen to prevent recurrence
- Drugs with highest hypoglycemia risk: insulin, sulfonylureas
