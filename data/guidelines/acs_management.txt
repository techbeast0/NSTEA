Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS) Management Guidelines
Source: ACC/AHA 2023 Clinical Practice Guidelines (summarized)

INITIAL ASSESSMENT
- All patients with suspected ACS should receive a 12-lead ECG within 10 minutes of arrival
- Troponin levels should be measured at presentation and repeated at 3-6 hours
- Oxygen therapy if SpO2 < 90%

STEMI MANAGEMENT
- Emergent reperfusion therapy (PCI preferred, door-to-balloon < 90 minutes)
- Dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT): aspirin 325mg loading + P2Y12 inhibitor
- If aspirin allergy: clopidogrel 600mg loading dose as alternative
- Anticoagulation: unfractionated heparin or enoxaparin
- Beta-blocker (oral) within 24 hours if no contraindications
- ACE inhibitor within 24 hours for anterior STEMI or EF < 40%
- High-intensity statin therapy

NSTEMI MANAGEMENT
- Risk stratification using TIMI or GRACE score
- Antiplatelet: aspirin + P2Y12 inhibitor (ticagrelor preferred)
- If aspirin allergy: clopidogrel as monotherapy or with ticagrelor
- Early invasive strategy for high-risk features
- Anticoagulation during hospitalization

CONTRAINDICATIONS
- NSAIDs (other than aspirin) are contraindicated in ACS — increase cardiovascular risk
- Non-selective beta-blockers contraindicated in asthma/severe COPD
- ACE inhibitors contraindicated in bilateral renal artery stenosis
- Thrombolytics contraindicated if recent major surgery, active bleeding, or stroke

DRUG ALLERGY CONSIDERATIONS
- Aspirin-allergic patients: use clopidogrel as antiplatelet alternative
- Heparin-induced thrombocytopenia: use bivalirudin
- Statin intolerance: try lower dose or alternative statin; consider ezetimibe
